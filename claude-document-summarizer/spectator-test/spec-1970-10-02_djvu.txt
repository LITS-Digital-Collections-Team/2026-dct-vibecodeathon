This issue of *The Spectator*, the student newspaper of Hamilton and Kirkland Colleges in Clinton, New York, dated October 2, 1970 (Volume 1, Number 3), covers a range of campus news, opinion, and cultural content.

A front-page article reports on a planned Tuesday meeting in the Chapel to redefine the role of the Hamilton-Kirkland Strike Committee, originally formed in May around a three-point platform opposing the war in Southeast Asia, campus complicity with military research, and repression of political prisoners. Another front-page piece describes the Free School of Clinton, co-founded by Steve Weisman and Stu Kestenbaum, beginning its second year with courses in arts and skills not offered in the regular curriculum, including pottery, photography, leather craft, and women's liberation.

Coverage of the Winter Study Program outlines approximately fifty projects available in January, including study-abroad options in London, Germany, Greece, and Paris, as well as exchange opportunities at Colgate, Kirkland, Lincoln, Skidmore, and Wells. Dean Winton Tolles is quoted noting no scholarship aid was available for the program.

A major feature reprints the progress report of the Trustee Ad Hoc Committee on Student Life, chaired by Howard M. Bingham. The report examines the social and residential system at Hamilton, including fraternity life, dining facilities (particularly Commons Dining Hall), and dormitory conditions (particularly Dunham Dormitory). The committee recommends creating real alternatives to fraternity membership, improving dining and housing facilities, and giving non-fraternity members preference in dormitory room allocation.

An editorial by Editor-in-Chief Ronald J. Bruck observes that fraternities are declining faster than anticipated, with freshman participation at architectural tours notably low. Other editorials address a proposed Honor Court amendment expanding penalty options for academic fraud and offer an apology for factual errors in a previous issue regarding Kirkland's governance structure.

Additional articles cover Kirkland's Class of 1972 enrollment status, the resignation of Superintendent of Buildings and Grounds John J. Letzelter, parking issues discussed by Director of Housing Gilbert J. Grout, a proposed faculty evaluation procedure involving student input, an article on Puyallup Indian fishing rights disputes in Washington state, a lawsuit against New Mexico National Guard officials, and sports coverage including football, soccer, and arts events including a Bruce Carter woodcut exhibition at Root Art Center and a review of Beethoven piano concertos.
