This issue of *The Spectator*, the student newspaper of Hamilton and Kirkland Colleges in Clinton, New York, dated May 6, 1970 (Volume 23, Number 27), is devoted primarily to an editorial statement titled "A Concerted Effort: Statement of Fact," written by Editor-in-Chief Ronald J. Bruck along with Jaime E. Yordan and James H. Higby. The editorial documents the student response at Hamilton College to the Cambodian crisis and expanding American military involvement in Southeast Asia.

The piece describes how, over approximately 48 hours, Hamilton students organized a strike and established seven workshops addressing various antiwar activities. These included: a Local Activities Coordinating Committee that contacted other Central New York colleges and planned a peaceful demonstration at Griffiss Air Force Base in Rome, New York, scheduled for Friday; outreach to 78 Utica-area churches for peace rallies in Oneida County, with committee co-chairman J.K. Hage ('72) cited; a mass media committee communicating with the United States Scholastic Press Association and striking colleges nationwide; a Campus Complicity Workshop researching college endowments and stockholder proxies as a protest mechanism, a plan adopted by the National Student Mobilization Committee; a committee proposing organized nationwide refusal of military induction by college seniors; a fund-raising committee that collected over $500; a draft counseling committee; and a Committee on Academic Alterations proposing to free students from end-of-semester academic obligations.

The issue also lists a schedule of events for May 6, including faculty teach-ins led by professors Warren Wright, Melvin Endy, Grant Jones, Norman Bowie, Robert Simon, Russell Blackwood, Eugene Lewis, and Lafayette Todd on topics such as conscientious objection, dissent, and war. A Hamilton College faculty meeting in Buttrick Hall and a student body meeting in the Chapel were also scheduled.

The editorial notes that Kirkland College voted to continue its academic program while encouraging antiwar expression. It calls upon the Hamilton faculty to suspend the normal academic schedule for the remaining two weeks of the semester so students could continue their organized activities.
