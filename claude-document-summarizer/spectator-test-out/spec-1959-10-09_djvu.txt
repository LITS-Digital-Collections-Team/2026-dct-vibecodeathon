This document is an issue of the Hamilton College Spectator, the student newspaper of Hamilton College in Clinton, New York, published in October 1959. The issue contains several articles on campus news, events, and commentary.

The lead article reports on a chapel lecture by William Griffith, a Hamilton alumnus, titled Thaw and Frost in Eastern Europe. Griffith discussed political cycles in Soviet satellite states following the death of Joseph Stalin, analysing the causes of the 1956 revolts in Poland and Hungary. He attributed the uprisings to mass alienation under communist rule, intellectual disillusionment with communism, nationalism, economic failure, and contact between intellectuals and the general population. Griffith also described Nikita Khrushchev's subsequent policies and concluded that the United States could do little directly in Eastern Europe beyond supporting dissident elements short of open revolt.

A second article covers a chapel lecture by Professor John A. Hutchinson of Columbia University on Saint Augustine's City of God, the first in the year's Morron lecture series. Hutchinson examined Augustine's theology, philosophy of history, and continuing relevance.

Other articles report on Hamilton's football loss to Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute and an upcoming game against Wagner College, a soccer loss to Rochester, cross-country and debate team activities, and intramural sports standings. The Student Senate's discussions concerning fraternity rushing rules, social probation of three fraternities, a proposed beer licence for the campus PX, and the appointment of various committees are also covered.

Additional items describe the appointment of fourteen new faculty members, the forthcoming dedication of the Thomas Brown Rudd Infirmary, a forthcoming novice debate tournament, a lecture on Oriental influence in American painting at the Root Art Center, and a screening of the Italian film Bicycle Thieves by the Foreign Films Society.
